## Step B: Determine Eligibility for Treasurer's Deed (3-Year Rule)

**Redemption Period:** 3 years from lien sale date.  
If not redeemed after 3 years, you may apply for a **Public Auction of a Certificate of Option for Treasurer's Deed**.

Confirm with the county that:  
- The lien has not been redeemed.  
- The redemption period has expired.  

Owners can still redeem **until** the Treasurer's Deed process is finalized.

- **$1,500.00** for properties with assessed value greater than $500.00
- Fee covers deed processing, title search, publication, mailing, and other auction expenses

**Application Steps:**
1. **FIRST:** Call (303) 679-2353 to confirm current procedures and waiting list status
2. Request the "Application for Public Auction of a Certificate of Option for Treasurer's Deed."
3. Provide your Certificate of Purchase (or certified copy).
4. Include any assignments, proof of endorsements, and ownership documentation.
5. Pay the $1,500 deposit (certified funds).
6. Submit to:
   **Clear Creek County Treasurer's Office**
   405 Argentine Street, Georgetown, CO 80444

**Processing time:** Approximately 5 months.
During this time, redemption may still occur by the property owner.

## Step D: Public Auction and Deed Issuance

- The county will conduct a **Treasurer's Deed Auction** for the Certificate of Option.  
- Rules: [Bidding Policy for Treasurer's Deed Auction](https://clearcreekcounty.us/1542/BIDDING-POLICY-FOR-TREASURERS-DEED-PUBLI)  
- If redeemed during auction window, funds are returned.  
- If no redemption, deed is issued and recorded after 10 business days.  

After issuance, record the Treasurer's Deed with the **Clear Creek County Clerk & Recorder**.

## Step E: Understanding Non-Marketable Deeds & Quiet Title

**üßæ Why Treasurer's Deeds are "Non-Marketable"**

A Treasurer's Deed gives you legal ownership but not clear (insurable) title right away.

- **Marketable title** means you can sell it, refinance it, or get title insurance easily
- **Non-marketable** means there may still be clouds on the title - other people could later challenge ownership (e.g., heirs, prior mortgagees, unknown owners who claim they didn't get proper notice)

**Why this happens in Colorado:**

The Treasurer's Deed process is administrative, not judicial. The county treasurer issues the deed after notices and waiting periods - but no court formally "clears" competing claims.

Under Colorado law, people who might have lost rights (e.g., prior owners) typically have up to 7 years to challenge the deed (C.R.S. ¬ß38-41-108, "limitations to recover lands sold for taxes").

**How investors fix this:**

**Option 1 - Wait it out**
- Hold the property for 7 years
- After that, Colorado's statute of limitations generally bars challenges to your deed (except in rare cases like fraud)

**Option 2 - File a quiet title action**
- A court proceeding asking a judge to confirm your ownership and eliminate other claims
- Costs vary ($2,500-$5,000+ depending on attorney and complexity)
- Once the court grants judgment, your title is fully marketable and insurable right away
- Many investors do this before reselling or developing the property
