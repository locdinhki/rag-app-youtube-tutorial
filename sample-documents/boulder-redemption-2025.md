# Boulder County Tax Lien Redemption Guidelines 2025

## Overview

This document outlines the tax lien redemption process for Boulder County, Colorado. Property owners who have had their property sold at a tax lien sale have the right to redeem their property within a specified period.

## Redemption Period

The redemption period in Boulder County is **three (3) years** from the date of the tax lien sale. During this time, the property owner may redeem the property by paying all delinquent taxes, interest, and fees.

### Key Dates

- Tax Lien Sale Date: Varies annually (typically in November)
- Redemption Deadline: 3 years from sale date
- Notice Period: 90 days before redemption period expires

## Redemption Requirements

To redeem a property, the property owner must:

1. Pay all delinquent taxes from the date of sale
2. Pay interest at the rate determined by the Treasurer
3. Pay all fees and costs associated with the sale
4. Submit payment during business hours at the Treasurer's Office

### Interest Rates

Interest accrues on tax liens at a rate determined by Colorado state law:
- Current rate: 9% per annum (subject to change)
- Interest compounds monthly
- Partial payments do not reduce interest on remaining balance

## Payment Methods

The Boulder County Treasurer's Office accepts the following payment methods for redemption:

- Cash (exact amount required)
- Cashier's check or money order
- Wire transfer (contact office for instructions)
- Personal checks NOT accepted for redemptions

### Business Hours

Monday - Friday: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Closed on county holidays

## Required Documentation

Property owners must provide:

1. Valid government-issued photo ID
2. Proof of ownership (deed or title)
3. Tax lien sale certificate number (if available)
4. Contact information for correspondence

## Notice Requirements

### First Notice
The Treasurer's Office will send a first notice to the property owner at least 90 days before the redemption period expires.

### Final Notice
A final notice will be sent 30 days before expiration, detailing:
- Total amount owed
- Exact deadline for redemption
- Consequences of non-redemption

## Consequences of Non-Redemption

If the property is not redeemed before the deadline:

1. Certificate holder may apply for Treasurer's Deed
2. Property ownership transfers to certificate holder
3. Former owner loses all rights to the property
4. No further opportunity for redemption exists

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I make partial payments?
No. The full redemption amount must be paid at once.

### What if I can't afford to redeem?
Contact the Treasurer's Office to discuss potential options or payment plans before the redemption period expires.

### Can someone else redeem on my behalf?
Yes, with proper authorization and documentation proving they have authority to act on your behalf.

### Is redemption guaranteed if I pay on time?
Yes, as long as payment is made before the deadline and all requirements are met.
